<Question> 
 In this study, did authors aim at studying Adults with other neurodevelopmental disabilities (not ASD) ?

<All Answers to this Question>

Final answers statistics: A total of 6 papers answered yes to this question,  whereas 90 papers answered no. Here is the information of the papers that answered yes: 


###
Citation: (Conti et al., 2020) 
Paper title: An empirical study on integrating a small humanoid robot to support the therapy of children with Autism Spectrum Disorder and Intellectual Disability 
Answer:Yes. The study included adult participants with intellectual disabilities.

###
Citation: (Lindsay, 2020) 
Paper title: Exploring Skills Gained Through a Robotics Program for Youth With Disabilities 
Answer:Yes. The study focused on youth with disabilities aged 9-14.

###
Citation: (Pennington et al., 2014) 
Paper title: Using Robot-Assisted Instruction to Teach Students with Intellectual Disabilities to Use Personal Narrative in Text Messages 
Answer:Yes. The participants were 3 adults (aged 19-21) with intellectual disabilities.

###
Citation: Reardon et al., 2018 
Paper title: Robots Can Teach Students With Intellectual Disabilities: Educational Benefits of Using Robotic and Augmented Reality Applications 
Answer:Yes, The study focused on college-age students with intellectual and developmental disabilities (I/DDs)

###
Citation: (Silvera-Tawil et al., 2018) 
Paper title: Talk to me: The role of human-robot interaction in Improving verbal communication skills in students with Autism or Intellectual Disability 
Answer:Yes, the study included secondary school students (13-19 years old) with intellectual disabilities

###
Citation: Zanatta et al. (2023) 
Paper title: Combining robot-assisted therapy with virtual reality or using it alone? A systematic review on health-related quality of life in neurological patients 
Answer:Yes, the study focused on adults with neurological conditions like stroke, multiple sclerosis, spinal cord injury, and Parkinson's disease
